General Course Summary

Pinellas Technical College's Applied Cybersecurity program (program Y100300) is designed to give students the tools of knowledge they need in order to successfully embark on cybersecurity-related careers and fields. In this program, faculty experienced in the industry will give students knowledge and skills that are both in-depth and based on the real world while also allowing students to earn up to four cybersecurity-related certifications

Students will learn how to install, upgrade, secure, and optimize operating systems, network equipment, and system software, identify system vulnerabilities and the causes of network intrusion; enact organizational prepardness capabilities against cyberattacks; and defned enterprises' networks against threats.

Completion of this course opens up the following careers for you:

  • Security Administrator
  • Cloud Engineer
  • Computer Defense Technician
  • Policy & Risk Management Specialist

Program Y100300

Cybersecurity Associate - Course CT50018
(600 Hours)
This course provides lecture, demonstration, and hands-on practice in IT fundamentals, including operating systems and PC components, networking fundamentals, including common networking models, communication protocols, cloud computing, risk management, and policy creation. Additionally, this course provides security fundamentals, including hacker culture, attack types, security vulnerabilities, malware, encryption, and defense strategies. Both CompTIA Security+ and pertinent Microsoft certification exam objectives will be covered in this course.
Information Security Manager - Course CTS0019
(150 Hours)
Includes cybersecurity risk mitigation planning; establishing a risk management framework; creating a corporate security policy; addressing process risks, contingency planning, and the essential elements of disaster recovery.
